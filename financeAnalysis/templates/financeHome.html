Screening stocks can help decide quickly using particular indicators to determine whether or not a stock is viable to buy, sell, or hold. Knowing a good entrypoint of when to invest can maximize profits. The following rules are utilized to see if a stock has a BuySignal.

  1. Current Price is greater than the 150 day simple moving average (SMA) which is greater than 200 day SMA
  2. 150 day simple moving average (SMA) > 200 day moving average
  3. 200 day SMA has been trending for 1 month
  4. Current Price is greater than the 50 day SMA
  5. Current Price is 30% Greater than 52 week low
  6. Current price is within 25% of 52 week high
  7. RSI (see below) is greater than 70
  8. Yield > 4%
  9. Earnings Per Share > 0
  10. Price/Earnings Ratio < 25

Relative Strength Index (RSI) is utilized to determine if a stock is overbought or oversold. By analyzing the average gain / average loss on a day-to-day basis and normalizing that data, we can see if a particular ticker is oversold and ready to be bought. The RSI oscillates between zero and 100. Traditionally, the RSI is considered overbought when above 70 and oversold when below 30 typically meaning people do not want this stock.
